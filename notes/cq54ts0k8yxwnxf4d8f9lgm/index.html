Generalized group ownership model

  • (Model). Generalized group ownership model.
    • (Generalization 1.) formalizing the shareholder into the matrix equation
      • If we include the shareholder in the adjacency matrix WWW, then WWW can be expressed as
        [0d1d2⋯dn0W11W12⋯W1n⋮W21⋱⋯W2n⋮⋮⋮⋱⋮0Wn1Wn2⋯Wnn]\begin{bmatrix}0 & d_1 & d_2 & \cdots & d_n\\ 0 & W_{11} & W_{12} & \cdots & W_{1n} \\ \vdots & W_{21} & \ddots & \cdots & W_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & W_{n1} & W_{n2} & \cdots & W_{nn} \end{bmatrix}⎣⎡​00⋮⋮0​d1​W11​W21​⋮Wn1​​d2​W12​⋱⋮Wn2​​⋯⋯⋯⋱⋯​dn​W1n​W2n​⋮Wnn​​⎦⎤​
      • The first column is entirely zero because it represents the ownership of the group of firms over the shareholder, which they do not own at all. The first column is d1,d2,...=dd_1, d_2, ... = dd1​,d2​,...=d, the row vector representing the ownership of the shareholder over the group.
      • The remaining parts of the matrix remains the same as the original adjacency matrix WGW^GWG.
      • Now vvv also contains the value of the shareholder.
      • This allows us to write directly: vG=(I−W)−1vv^G = (I-W)^{-1}vvG=(I−W)−1v as before, except now the group value vector contains the group value of the shareholder.
      • Generalizing (2) from above, we also have W^=W+W^W\hat{W}=W+\hat{W}WW^=W+W^W where W^\hat{W}W^ is the integrated ownership matrix of the shareholder plus every other firm.
        • Interpretation: in scalar form this can be written Wij^=Wij+∑kWikW^kj\hat{W_{ij}}=W_{ij}+\sum_{k}W_{ik}\hat{W}_{kj}Wij​^​=Wij​+∑k​Wik​W^kj​ - the integrated ownership of a firm is the direct ownership WijW_{ij}Wij​ plus the integrated ownership of every firm towards firm jjj, weighted by the directed ownership of firm iii towards those firms.
        • (Theorem.) W^=W(1−W)−1\hat{W}=W(1-W)^{-1}W^=W(1−W)−1. Perron-Frobenius theorem ensures this matrix inverse exists
        • (Theorem.) WvG=W^vWv^G=\hat{W}vWvG=W^v and thus vG=WvG+v=W^v+vv^G = Wv^G+v=\hat{W}v+vvG=WvG+v=W^v+v.
          • (Proof.) W^v=W(1−W)−1v=WvG\hat{W}v=W(1-W)^{-1}v=Wv^GW^v=W(1−W)−1v=WvG.
          • Interpretation: the group value weighted by the direct ownership percentages, WvGWv^GWvG, is equal to the intrinsic value weighted by the integrated ownership percentages, W^v\hat{W}vW^v.
    • (Generalization 2). Integrating the shareholder into the business group.
      • The shareholder may also be owned by a firm in the group, i.e. the shareholder may also be part of the group.
      • Formally, this would mean that the first column of the adjacency matrix of the group is nonzero: in the following adjacency matrix of the group
        [W11W12...W1nW21W22...W2n⋮⋱...⋮Wn1Wn2...Wnn]\begin{bmatrix}W_{11} & W_{12} & ... & W_{1n} \\ W_{21} & W_{22} & ... & W_{2n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & ... & \vdots \\ W_{n1} & W_{n2} & ... & W_{nn} \end{bmatrix}⎣⎡​W11​W21​⋮Wn1​​W12​W22​⋱Wn2​​............​W1n​W2n​⋮Wnn​​⎦⎤​
        The term WijW_{ij}Wij​ represents the direct ownership of firm iii in firm jjj, and if we take firm 111 to be the shareholder, then all Wi1≠0W_{i1} \neq 0Wi1​=0.
