Topology of the World Trade Web

Serrano and Boguna

The World Trade Network satisfies the properties of a complex network.

Definition of a complex network

Definition. A complex network demonstrates the following properties:

  • A scale-free degree distribution, where the fraction of nodes with degree kkk, P(k)P(k)P(k), follows an inverse power-law distribution P(k)∝k−γP(k) \propto k^{-\gamma}P(k)∝k−γ, where 2<γ≤32<\gamma\leq 32<γ≤3.
    • Interpretation. This implies that there are far fewer nodes with a higher degree than a lower degree; the network will be centered around a few "hubs", i.e. nodes which are much more well-connected than others.
  • The small-world property, where the average path length between any pair of nodes grows logarithmically with the graph size.
    • Interpretation. The size of the graph could be very large (say 1000 nodes), but any two randomly selected nodes will have a small distance on average (e.g. 3) due to the logarithmic relationship between graph size and distance.
  • A high clustering coefficient, defined for a node iii to be
    2niki(ki−1)=ni(ki(ki−1)2)\frac{2n_i}{k_i(k_i-1)} = \frac{n_i}{(\frac{k_i(k_i-1)}{2})}ki​(ki​−1)2ni​​=(2ki​(ki​−1)​)ni​​
    where nin_ini​ is the number of pairs of nodes connected to iii that are themselves connected, and kik_iki​ is the degree of iii, i.e. the number of nodes connected to iii
    • Interpretation. (ki(ki−1)2)(\frac{k_i(k_i-1)}{2})(2ki​(ki​−1)​) is the total number of possible pairs of nodes connected to iii (= (ki2)k_i\choose 2(2ki​​)); thus, the clustering coefficient is the ratio between the pairs which are connected to the total number of pairs.
  • Recently, a degree-degree correlation has also been added to the list of properties: the network should be either assortative, i.e. high-degree vertices are connected to other high-degree vertices, or disassortiative, i.e. high-degree vertices are connected to low-degree vertices

The World Trade Web is a complex network

Method. The adjacency matrix for the World Trade Web in this paper is unweighted and is defined for countries iii, jjj to be

Aij=11+δIij,Eij(Iij+Eij)A_{ij} = \frac{1}{1+\delta_{I_{ij},E_{ij}}}(I_{ij}+E_{ij})Aij​=1+δIij​,Eij​​1​(Iij​+Eij​)

where IijI_{ij}Iij​ is 111 if there are imports to iii from jjj beyond a certain threshold, and EijE_{ij}Eij​ is 111 if there are exports from iii to jjj beyond a certain threshold. δ\deltaδ denotes the Kronecker delta function.

Interpretation. AijA_{ij}Aij​ is zero if there are no imports or exports between iii and jjj at all, but one otherwise (given the Kronecker delta). It thus represents whether there are trade relationships between iii and jjj.

Consequently, the adjacency matrix addressed in this paper is symmetrical.

  • There are 179 vertices and 7510 directed links.
  • The average degree is 30.9.

Takeaways.

  • Reciprocity: the fraction of trade relationships which are reciprocal is 0.61; this is (apparently) enough to think of the global trade network as reciprocal enough to be undirected (?)
  • In- and out-degree correlation. If in-degree is defined as importing nations and out-degree is exporting nations, the in-out correlation is 0.91.
  • If the network is symmetrized based on the above definition, the average degree ⟨k⟩\langle k \rangle⟨k⟩ is 43, which is very high; if the network is instead symmetrized based on bi-directionality, i.e. Aij=1A_{ij}=1Aij​=1 only if both import and export links exist, then ⟨k⟩\langle k \rangle⟨k⟩ is only 18.
  • Degree distribution. The proportion of the degree kkk peaks at k≈20k \approx 20k≈20 and decays according to a power law distribution P(k)∝k−2.6P(k) \propto k^{-2.6}P(k)∝k−2.6 after that; the network is thus scale-free for k>20k > 20k>20, but not below that.
  • Degree is positively correlated with country per capita GDP (0.65 correlation); some countries, like China, Brazil, and Russia, are relatively low in per-capita GDP but high in degree.
  • Clustering distribution. The clustering coefficient ccc is positively correlated with kkk by c∝k−0.7c \propto k^{-0.7}c∝k−0.7. The average clustering coefficient is 0.65, which is 2.7 times greater than the random expectation.
  • Degree-degree correlation. Defined formally as P(k′∣k)P(k'|k)P(k′∣k), the probability of the degree of a node being k′k'k′ conditioned upon the degree of a connected node being kkk. This can be measured by average nearest neighbor degree (ANND), the average degree of a node's connected given that it has a certain degree. If there is no degree-degree correlation, then ANND is unaffected by degree; if there is a correlation, then ANND is dependent on degree. A power law decay ANND∝k−0.5±0.05ANND \propto k^{-0.5 \pm 0.05}ANND∝k−0.5±0.05 was found; this indicates disassortativity (ANND inversely propotional to degree; high-degree nodes connected to low-degree ones).
  • Overall topology.
    • Size 179, average degree 43, average path length 1.8.
    • Reciprocity. In-degree and out-degree correlation (unsymmetrized) is 0.91; highly reciprocal.
    • Degree distribution. Peaks at k=20k=20k=20; follows scale-free distribution k−γk^{-\gamma}k−γ with γ=2.6\gamma = 2.6γ=2.6 affterwards. A few large hubs with many small countries with little trade.
